The Project

Participating in our study is easy and informative, you'll be asked to read the ethics briefing form and provide your consent to join the study.

Next, we'll gather some basic demographic information to better understand our participant group. After that, you'll take a quiz to assess your current ability to identify fake news; don't worry too much, this is just to get a baseline reading and all results will be anonymised.

We'll then start the tutorial in which you will watch a series of short videos which will help you to learn valuable skills that will help you to better understand and detect fake news.

Once you have watched the video, you will either be taken to a summary page, or you will get the chance to take a short quiz on the material from the tutorial; if you do see the quiz, please make sure you complete it before moving to the next topic.

Once you have completed the tutorial, as well as any relevant quizzes, you will be asked to retake the initial quiz.

After this has been completed, for those who are interested, there's an optional debriefing session, where we'll a series of multiple choice questions about your experience.

If you have any questions about the project, process, data handling or anything else please get in touch.
